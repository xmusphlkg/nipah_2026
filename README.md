
The Empty-Nest Egret

Author: BRID

Once, on the riverbank, a community of birds worked together—small waterhens dived for fish, swallows stitched mud nests, and kingfishers kept watch over the reeds. Their days were full of toil, and their nests and tables grew rich from honest labor.

One season an egret arrived and chose the brightest branch. It never waded into the shallows, never mended a nest; instead it spoke at great length in the assembly, claiming wisdom and distant deeds.

When harvest time came, the diligent birds piled fish and grain into high mounds. The egret, needing nothing more than a pen and a page, signed its name on the list and took its share of the feast as if it had earned it.
